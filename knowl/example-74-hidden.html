Consider the ODE

\begin{equation*} (t-2)y^{(4)}+\sin ty^{\prime\prime\prime}+\ln ty=\sqrt{t+5}. \end{equation*}

Find the intervals where you are guaranteed a unique solution to this ODE by the Uniqueness and Existence Theorem.

Rewriting, we have

\begin{equation*} y^{(4)}+\frac{\sin t}{(t-2)}y^{\prime\prime\prime}+\frac{\ln t}{(t-2)}y=\frac{\sqrt{t+5}}{(t-2)} \end{equation*}

and

  • \(\frac{\sin t}{(t-2)}\) is comtinuous when \(t\neq2\)
  • \(\frac{\ln t}{(t-2)}\) is continuous when \(t>0\) and \(t\neq2\text{,}\) and
  • \(\frac{\sqrt{t+5}}{(t-2)}\) is continuous when \(t\geq-5\) and \(t\neq2\text{.}\)

Making a number line we see that all three functions are continuous when either on the interval \((0,2)\) or \((2,\infty)\text{.}\)
