Example 4.2.2.

Consider

\begin{equation*} y^{\prime\prime\prime}+4y^{\prime}=t+\sin(4t). \end{equation*}

Find the general form of \(y_{p}\text{.}\)

Step 1: We find \(y_{h}\text{:}\) Solve

\begin{align*} r^{3}+4r \amp =0\\ r\left(r^{2}+4\right) \amp =0 \end{align*}

so that \(y_{h}=c_{1}+c_{2}\cos2t+c_{3}\sin2t\text{.}\)

Step 2: Find \(y_{p}\text{:}\) We first guess \(y_{p}=At+B+C\cos(4t)+D\sin(4t)\text{.}\) But \(B\) is already in \(y_{c}\) as \(c_{1}\text{.}\) So instead make the second guess \(y_{p}=t\left(At+B\right)+C\cos(4t)+D\sin(4t)\) which is correct.
