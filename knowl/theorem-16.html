Theorem 6.4.6. Second translation theorem.

If \(F(s)=\mathcal{L}\left\{ f(t)\right\} \) exists for \(s>a\geq0\) and \(c>0\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \mathcal{L}\left\{ e^{ct}f\left(t\right)\right\} =F(s-c),\,\,\,\,s>a+c. \end{equation*}

Conversely, if \(f(t)=\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{ F(s-c)\right\} \text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} e^{ct}f(t)=\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{ F(s-c)\right\} . \end{equation*}
