Mathematically, we can translate this into a differential equation. Let \(T\) be the temperature of the object at time \(t\text{,}\) and \(A\) be the ambient temperature. Then

\begin{equation*} \frac{dT}{dt} = C(T - A), \end{equation*}

where \(C\) is the constant of proportionality.
