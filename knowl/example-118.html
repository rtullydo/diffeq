Example 6.4.11.

Take the Inverse Laplace Transform of:\({\displaystyle F(s)=\frac{1}{s^{2}}+\frac{e^{-6s}}{\left(s-2\right)^{3}}}\text{.}\)

We know \(\mathcal{L}\left\{ t\right\} =\frac{1}{s^{2}}\text{,}\) \(\mathcal{L}\left\{ t^{2}\right\} =\frac{2}{s^{3}}\) and \(\mathcal{L}\left\{ e^{ct}f\left(t\right)\right\} =F(s-c)\) and \(\mathcal{L}\left\{ u_{c}(t)f\left(t-c\right)\right\} =e^{-cs}F(s)\) hence

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{ F(s)\right\} \amp =t+\frac{1}{2}\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{ \frac{2e^{-6s}}{\left(s-2\right)^{3}}\right\} \\ \amp =t+\frac{1}{2}u_{6}(t)e^{2(t-6)}(t-6)^{2}. \end{align*}
