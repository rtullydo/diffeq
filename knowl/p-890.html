A point \(x=x_0\) is called an ordinary point for a homogeneous second order differential equation of the form

\begin{equation*} y'' + P(x)y' + Q(x)y = 0 \end{equation*}

if \(P\) and \(Q\) are analytic at \(x_0\text{.}\) (Typical functions for \(P,Q\) are trig functions, exponentials, polynomials, and rational functions, which are analytic away from asymptotes).
