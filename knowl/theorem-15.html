Theorem 6.4.5. First translation theorem.

If \(F(s)=\mathcal{L}\left\{ f(t)\right\} \) exists for \(s>a\geq0\) and \(c>0\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \mathcal{L}\left\{ u_{c}(t)f\left(t-c\right)\right\} =e^{-cs}\mathcal{L}\left\{ f(t)\right\} =e^{-cs}F(s), \end{equation*}

Conversely, if \(f(t)=\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{ F(s)\right\} \text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} u_{c}(t)f\left(t-c\right) =\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{ e^{-cs}F(s)\right\} . \end{equation*}
