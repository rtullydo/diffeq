Example 1.1.5. Finding an equilibrium solution.
Find the equilibrium solution of the following equation. Suppose that
\begin{equation*} y' = y^3 + y^2 - 6y. \end{equation*}
For what values of \(y(0) = y_0\) is \(y(t)\) equilibrium, increasing, or decreasing? Factor to get
\begin{equation*} y' = y(y-2)(y+3), \end{equation*}
and create a sign chart (like in calculus!). The equilibrium solutions are \(y = -3, y=0,\) and \(y=2\text{.}\) We can observe that solutions decrease for \(y_0 \in (-\infty, -3)\cup(0,2)\) and that they increase for \((-3,0)\cup(2,\infty)\text{.}\)
