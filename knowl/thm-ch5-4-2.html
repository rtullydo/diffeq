Theorem 5.4.4. Real and distinct eigenvalues.

Suppose \(A\) is a matrix with distinct, real eigenvalues \(\lambda_{1},\lambda_{2}\) with corresponding eigenvectors \(\mathbf{v}_{1},\mathbf{v}_{2}\text{,}\) respectively. Then the general solution of the system

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{\prime}=A\mathbf{x} \end{equation*}

is

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}(t)=c_{1}e^{\lambda_{1}t}\mathbf{v}_{1}+c_{2}e^{\lambda_{2}t}\mathbf{v}_{2}. \end{equation*}

Moreover, the solutions \(\mathbf{x}^{(1)}(t)=e^{\lambda_{1}t}\mathbf{v}_{1},\mathbf{x}^{(2)}(t)=e^{\lambda_{2}t}\mathbf{v}_{2}\) are linearly independent.
