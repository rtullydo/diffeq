Exercise 3.

Consider the linear system

\begin{equation*} \frac{d\mathbf{x}}{dt}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} -2 \amp -1\\ 2 \amp -5 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x} \end{equation*}

  1. Check that the two functions are solutions to the system. If they are not solutions, then stop and do not do parts (b) and (c).

    \begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{(1)}(t)=\left(\begin{array}{c} e^{-3t}-2e^{-4t}\\ e^{-3t}-4e^{-4t} \end{array}\right)\text{ and }\mathbf{x}^{(2)}(t)=\left(\begin{array}{c} 2e^{-3t}+e^{-4t}\\ 2e^{-3t}+2e^{-4t} \end{array}\right) \end{equation*}

  2. Are \(\mathbf{x}^{(1)}(t)\) and \(\mathbf{x}^{(2)}(t)\) linearly independent? If they are not, then stop and do not move on to part (c).

  3. Find the general solution of

    \begin{equation*} \frac{d\mathbf{x}}{dt}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} -2 \amp -1\\ 2 \amp -5 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x}. \end{equation*}
