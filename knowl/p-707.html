Step 1: Take \(\mathcal{L}\) of both sides and solve \(\mathcal{L}\left\{ y\right\} \text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} 4\left(s^{2}\mathcal{L}\left\{ y\right\} -sy(0)-y^{\prime}(0)\right)+4\left(s\mathcal{L}\left\{ y\right\} -y(0)\right)+17\mathcal{L}\left\{ y\right\} =\mathcal{L}\left\{ g(t)\right\} \end{equation*}

and plugging in the initial conditions we have

\begin{equation*} \mathcal{L}\left\{ y\right\} \left(4s^{2}+4s+17\right)=\mathcal{L}\left\{ g(t)\right\} \end{equation*}

so that

\begin{equation*} \mathcal{L}\left\{ y\right\} =\frac{\mathcal{L}\left\{ g(t)\right\} }{4s^{2}+4s+17} \end{equation*}
