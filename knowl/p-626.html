Solution: The discontinuity points are \(t=0,1,2,3\text{.}\) When \(0\leq t\lt 1\text{,}\) the function will be \(f(t)=tu_{0}(t)+\cdots\text{.}\) Our goal is to figure out the rest. When \(1\leq t\lt 2\text{,}\) the function will be \(f(t)=tu_{0}(t)+\boldsymbol{?}\cdot u_{1}(t)+\cdots=t-1\text{,}\) hence

\begin{equation*} t+?=t-1\,\,\,\implies?=-1. \end{equation*}

Hence \(f(t)=tu_{0}(t)-1\cdot u_{1}(t)+\cdots\) When \(2\leq \lt 3\text{,}\) the function will be \(f(t)=tu_{0}(t)-1\cdot u_{1}(t)+\boldsymbol{?}u_{2}(t)+\cdots=t-2\text{,}\) hence

\begin{equation*} t-1+?=t-2\,\,\,\implies?=-1. \end{equation*}

Hence \(f(t)=tu_{0}(t)-1\cdot u_{1}(t)-1u_{2}(t)+\cdots\) When \(t\geq3\text{,}\) the function will be \(f(t)=tu_{0}(t)-1\cdot u_{1}(t)-1u_{2}(t)+\boldsymbol{?}u_{3}(t)\cdots=0\text{,}\) hence

\begin{equation*} t-1-1+?=0\,\,\,\implies?=2-t \end{equation*}

Thus

\begin{equation*} f(t)=tu_{0}(t)-u_{1}(t)-u_{2}(t)+(2-t)u_{3}(t). \end{equation*}
