This might seem a bit complicated to parse, but the idea is very easy to use in practice. Consider the equation

\begin{equation*} x^2 + 5x - 6 = 0. \end{equation*}

Then we have that the roots are \(5/2 \pm z\text{.}\) Since the roots must multiply to \(c\text{,}\) we get

\begin{gather*} (5/2 + z)(5/2 - z) = -6\\ \Rightarrow 25/4 - z^2 = -6 \\ \Rightarrow z^2 = 49/4 \\ \Rightarrow z = \pm 7/2 \end{gather*}

Then the roots are \(-5/2 \pm 7/2\text{,}\) so \(R = -6\) and \(S = 1\text{,}\) which means the equation factors as \((x + 6)(x -1) = 0\text{.}\)
