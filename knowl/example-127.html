Example 6.3.4.

Find the inverse transform of \(F(s)=\frac{2s-8}{s^{2}-4s+5}\text{.}\)

Note that we can't factor \(s^{2}-4s+5\) with real roots, thus we will complete the square.

Completing the square: Suppose we have \(s^{2}+bs+c\text{,}\) then the trick is to ADD/SUBTRACT \(\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^{2}\text{,}\) and the polynomials will become \(s^{2}+bs+c=\left(s+\frac{b}{2}\right)^{2}-\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^{2}+c\text{.}\) To complete the square for \(s^{2}-4s+5\text{:}\) Then \(b=-4\) hence we add/subtract \(\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^{2}=\left(\frac{-4}{2}\right)^{2}=4\text{.}\) Thus

\begin{align*} s^{2}-4s+5 \amp =s^{2}-4s+4+1\\ \amp =\left(s-2\right)^{2}+1 \end{align*}

Going back to the problem of find the Laplace Transform we have that

\begin{align*} F(s) \amp =\frac{2s-8}{\left(s-2\right)^{2}+1}=\frac{2\left(s-2\right)}{\left(s-2\right)^{2}+1}-4\frac{1}{\left(s-2\right)^{2}+1} \end{align*}

We can apply these by separating \(F(s)\) into pieces like this:=

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{ F(s)\right\} \amp =\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{ \frac{2s-8}{\left(s-2\right)^{2}+1}\right\} \\ \amp =\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{ \frac{2\left(s-2\right)}{\left(s-2\right)^{2}+1}\right\} -4\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left\{ \frac{1}{\left(s-2\right)^{2}+1}\right\} \\ \amp =2e^{2t}\cos t-4e^{2t}\sin t \end{align*}
