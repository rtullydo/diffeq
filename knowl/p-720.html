If \(f,g\) are two function where \(\mathcal{L}\) exists for \(s>a_{1}\) and \(s>a_{2}\text{,}\) respectively, Then

\begin{equation*} \mathcal{L}\left\{ f(t)\pm g(t)\right\} =\mathcal{L}\left\{ f(t))\right\} \pm\mathcal{L}\left\{ g(t)\right\} ,\,\,\,s>\max\left\{ a_{1},a_{2}\right\} , \end{equation*}

and We have for \(c\in\mathbb{R}\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \mathcal{L}\left\{ cf(t))\right\} =c\mathcal{L}\left\{ f(t))\right\} . \end{equation*}
