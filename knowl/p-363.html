These roots are distinct, so we can apply the form of solutions we developed in the previous section. So for the root \(r_1 = \alpha + i\beta\text{,}\) the solution is of the form

\begin{align*} y(t) \amp =e^{r_1t}=e^{\left(\alpha +i\beta \right)t}=e^{\alpha t}e^{i\beta t}\\ \amp =e^{\alpha t}\left(\cos\left(\beta t\right)+i\sin\left(\beta t\right)\right)\\ \amp =e^{\alpha t}\cos\left(\beta t\right)+ie^{\alpha t}\sin\left(\beta t\right)\\ \amp =u(t)+iv(t) \end{align*}

where \(u(t)=e^{\alpha t}\cos\left(\beta t\right)\) is the real part and \(v(t)=e^{\alpha t}\sin\left(\beta t\right)\) is the imaginary part.
