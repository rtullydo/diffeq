Exercise 6.

Classify the following equations as ODEs or PDEs.

  1. \(\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dt}=2yt\)
  2. \(\frac{\partial u}{\partial t}=\frac{\partial^{2}u}{\partial x^{2}}\)

    Fun Fact: This particular PDE is a very famous PDE and is called the heat equation. It models the flow of heat in a medium over time.

  3. \(\frac{\partial^{2}u}{\partial t^{2}}=\frac{\partial^{2}u}{\partial x^{2}}+\frac{\partial^{2}u}{\partial y^{2}}\)

    Fun Fact: This particular PDE is a very famous PDE and is called the wave equation. This PDE along with boundary conditions, describes the amplitude and phase of the wave.

  4. \(\displaystyle x\frac{d^{2}y}{dx^{2}}=y\frac{dy}{dx}+x^{2}y\)
  5. \(\displaystyle 2y^{\prime\prime}-y^{\prime}+y=0\)
