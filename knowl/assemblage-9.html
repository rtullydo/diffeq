Eigenvalue/Eigenvectors.

Given a matrix \(A\text{,}\) a number \(\lambda\) is called an eigenvalue of \(A\) if there is a nonzero vector \(\mathbf{v}\) such that

\begin{equation*} A\mathbf{v}=\lambda\mathbf{v}. \end{equation*}

The corresponding vector \(\mathbf{v}\) is called an eigenvector of the eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{.}\)
