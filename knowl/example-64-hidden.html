Find the general form of a particular solution of
\begin{equation*} y^{\prime\prime}-2y^{\prime}-3y=5te^{-t}. \end{equation*}

Step 1: Find \(y_{h}\) which is \(y_{h}=c_{1}e^{-t}+c_{2}e^{3t}\text{.}\)

Step 2: Using our table our first guess will be

\begin{equation*} y_{p}=\left(At+B\right)e^{-t} \end{equation*}

since \(At+B\) is the general form of a one degree polynomial. But this doesn't work because \(Be^{-t}\) is included in the \(y_{h}\) as \(c_{1}e^{-t}\)

Step 2 (Second guess): Now guess

\begin{equation*} y_{p}=t\left(At+B\right)e^{-t} \end{equation*}

so that both \(At^{2}e^{-t}\) and \(Bte^{-t}\) are different than the terms in \(y_{h}\text{.}\)
