Find the Laplace transform of

\begin{equation*} h(t)=\int_{0}^{t}\sin\left(t-\tau\right)\cos\tau d\tau \end{equation*}

Use \(f=\sin t\) and \(g=\cos t\) and we know that by the Theorem

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}\left\{ \int_{0}^{t}\sin\left(t-\tau\right)\cos\tau d\tau\right\} \amp =\mathcal{L}\left\{ \sin t\right\} \mathcal{L}\left\{ \cos t\right\} \\ \amp =\frac{1}{s^{2}+1}\cdot\frac{s}{s^{2}+1}\\ \amp =\frac{s}{(s^{2}+1)^{2}}. \end{align*}
