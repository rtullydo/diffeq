Convolutions have nice properties: We can treat \(\star\) almost like multiplication of functions:

  • \(f\star g=g\star f\) (commutative)
  • \(f\star\left(g_{1}+g_{2}\right)=f\star g_{1}+f\star g_{2}\) (distributive)
  • \(\left(f\star g\right)\star h=f\star\left(g\star h\right)\) (associative)
  • \(f\star0=0\star f=0\text{.}\)

However it doesn't have all the properties of ordinary multiplication. In particular, \(\left(f\star1\right)\neq1\star f\text{.}\)
