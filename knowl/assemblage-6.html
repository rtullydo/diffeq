Given a second order linear differential equation

\begin{equation*} a(t) y'' + b(t) y' + c(t) y = r(t) \end{equation*}

with a known solution \(y_1\text{,}\) a second solution is given by

\begin{equation*} y_2 = v(t)y_1. \end{equation*}

This substitution will reduce the order of the equation from second to first in terms of \(w = v'\text{.}\)
