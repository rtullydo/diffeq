In general, we should be suspicious of integrals involving infinity as a limit of integration. Why should we believe that such an integral is likely to converge? The key observation is that the exponential function \(e^{-st}\) tends to \(0\) very quickly. In fact exponentials dominate all polynomials, which is going to mean that the functions most commonly used in differential equations (polynomials, trig functions, and exponentials with domain restrictions) are all reasonably controlled by the exponential function \(e^{-st}\text{.}\)
