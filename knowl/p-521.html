Step 3: Use \(x_{i}^{\prime}=\text{Rate in }-\text{Rate out}\text{.}\) We first get

\begin{align*} x_{2}^{\prime}(t) \amp =\left(\begin{array}{c} \mbox{concentration}\\ \mbox{of salt coming in} \end{array}\right)\times\mbox{Rate}-\left(\begin{array}{c} \mbox{concentration}\\ \mbox{of salt coming out} \end{array}\right)\times\mbox{Rate} \\ \amp =\frac{x_{1}(t)}{30}\frac{\text{g}}{L}\cdot4\frac{\text{L}}{\text{min}}-\frac{x_{2}(t)}{20+(4-4)t}\cdot4\frac{\text{L}}{\text{min}}\\ \amp =\frac{4x_{1}(t)}{30}-\frac{4x_{2}(t)}{20},\,\,\,\,\,x_{2}(0)=0 \end{align*}

Putting it together, the system we have is

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} x_{1}^{\prime}=12-\frac{2}{15}x_{1} \amp x_{1}(0)=6.\\ x_{2}^{\prime}=\frac{4}{30}x_{1}-\frac{1}{5}x_{2} \amp x_{2}(0)=0 \end{cases} \end{equation*}
