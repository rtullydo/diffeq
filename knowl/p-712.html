Solution: We compute

\begin{align*} \mathcal{L}\left\{ e^{9t}\right\} \amp =\int_{0}^{\infty}f(t)e^{-st}dt=\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{9t}e^{-st}dt\\ \amp =\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{(9-s)t}dt\\ \amp =\frac{1}{9-s}\left[e^{(9-s)t}\right]_{t=0}^{t=\infty}\\ \amp =\frac{1}{9-s}\left[\lim_{b\to\infty}e^{(9-s)b}-e^{0}\right] \end{align*}

but since

\begin{equation*} \lim_{b\to\infty}e^{(9-s)b}=\begin{cases} \infty \amp a-s>0\\ 0 \amp a-s\lt 0 \end{cases} \end{equation*}

then

\begin{equation*} \mathcal{L}\left\{ e^{9t}\right\} =\begin{cases} \frac{1}{s-9} \amp s>9\\ \text{not defined} \amp s\lt 9 \end{cases}. \end{equation*}
