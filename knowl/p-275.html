Frequently, when given a function \(z = f(x,y)\text{,}\) we wish to know the direction of greatest slope or rate of change. For example, if \(z = f(x,y)\) represents the height of a mountain, the direction that water flows downhill will be in the direction of sleepest descent. We can use the partial derivatives to define a vector field that at each point \((x,y)\) gives the direction of greatest slope of the graph of \(f\text{.}\)
