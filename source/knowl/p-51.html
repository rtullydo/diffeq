Step 4: Integrate and solve for \(y(t)\) (don't forget the constant \(C\) in this step, which is very important!)

\begin{align*} \int\frac{d\left[\frac{1}{t^{3}}y\right]}{dt}=\int t^{2}dt+C \amp \iff \amp \frac{1}{t^{3}}y=\frac{t^{3}}{3}+C\\ \amp \iff \amp y(t)=\frac{t^{6}}{3}+Ct^{3}. \end{align*}
