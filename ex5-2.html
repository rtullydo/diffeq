Exercises Exercises

1.

Let \(A=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 \amp 2\\ 5 \amp 3 \end{array}\right)\text{,}\) \(\mathbf{x}=\left(\begin{array}{c} -2\\ 1 \end{array}\right)\) and \(\mathbf{y}=\left(\begin{array}{c} 3\\ 5 \end{array}\right)\text{,}\) find \(A\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) \(4\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) and \(\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{y}\text{.}\)

2.

Turn the following system of first order equations into matrix-product form:

  1. Given by:

    \begin{equation*} \begin{cases} x_{1}^{\prime}=3x_{2}\\ x_{2}^{\prime}=9x_{1}-3x_{2} \end{cases} \end{equation*}

  2. Given by:

    \begin{equation*} \begin{cases} x_{1}^{\prime}=-x_{1}+2x_{2}\\ x_{2}^{\prime}=7x_{1}+5x_{2} \end{cases} \end{equation*}
3.

Turn the following vector valued ODE into a system of first order equations.

  1. Given by:

    \begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 3 \amp -2\\ 3 \amp 17 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x} \end{equation*}

    where \(\boldsymbol{x}(t)=\left(\begin{array}{c} x_{1}(t)\\ x_{2}(t) \end{array}\right)\)

  2. Given by:

    \begin{equation*} \boldsymbol{Y}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 0 \amp 4\\ 7 \amp 3 \end{array}\right)\boldsymbol{Y} \end{equation*}

    where \(\boldsymbol{Y}(t)=\left(\begin{array}{c} x(t)\\ y(t) \end{array}\right)\text{.}\)

4.

Find the equilibrium solutions of the following system:

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 \amp 2\\ 1 \amp 3 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x}. \end{equation*}
5.

Find the equilibrium solutions of the following system:

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 3 \amp -1\\ 9 \amp -3 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x}. \end{equation*}
6.

Check if the following vector functions satisfy the following differential equations.

  1. Where

    \begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 3 \amp -2\\ 2 \amp -2 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x}\,\,\,\,\mathbf{x}(t)=\left(\begin{array}{c} 4e^{2t}\\ 2e^{2t} \end{array}\right). \end{equation*}

  2. Where

    \begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 \amp 3\\ 2 \amp 2 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x},\,\,\,\,\mathbf{x}(t)=\left(\begin{array}{c} 1\\ 2 \end{array}\right)e^{3t}. \end{equation*}
7.

Consider the following differential equation

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 \amp 4\\ 2 \amp 3 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x}, \end{equation*}

with corresponding direction field:

  1. Using the direction field draw a Phase portrait. (A Phase portrait is graph a several possible different solutions of the ODE)

  2. Consider the following IVP:

    \begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 \amp 4\\ 2 \amp 3 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x},\,\,\,\,\,\mathbf{x}(0)=\left(\begin{array}{c} -2\\ 1.5 \end{array}\right) \end{equation*}

    draw the unique soluton to this IVP and use it to predict long term behavior of \(\lim_{t\to\infty}x_{1}(t)\) and \(\lim_{t\to\infty}x_{2}(t)\text{.}\)

8.

Consider the following differential equation

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 \amp -4\\ 1 \amp -2 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x}, \end{equation*}

with corresponding direction field:

  1. Using the direction field draw a Phase portrait. (A Phase portrait is graph a several possible different solutions of the ODE)

  2. Consider the following IVP:

    \begin{equation*} \mathbf{x}^{\prime}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 \amp -4\\ 1 \amp -2 \end{array}\right)\mathbf{x},\,\,\,\,\,\mathbf{x}(0)=\left(\begin{array}{c} -2.5\\ 0 \end{array}\right) \end{equation*}

    draw the unique soluton to this IVP and use it to predict long term behavior of \(\lim_{t\to\infty}x_{1}(t)\) and \(\lim_{t\to\infty}x_{2}(t)\text{.}\)

  3. Try to draw what the graphs of \(x_{1}(t)\) and \(x_{2}(t)\) are individually as functions of \(t\text{,}\) for the same IVP above.
